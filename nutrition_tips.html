* To reduce sodium consumption when using canned vegetables, it is important to rinse them thoroughly to wash away excess salt.

*When selecting canned goods, it is always helpful to avoid dented cans because damage can compromise the seal, potentially leading to bacterial contamination.

* When selecting canned fruits to avoid excess sugar, look for those packed "in water" instead of "in 100% juice". Especially the ones in syrup.

Myths vs. Facts

What are common myths?

"Carbs are bad for you."

Fact: the body needs healthy carbs like oats and fruits for energy.

"Fat makes you fat."

Fact: Healthy fats (avocado, nuts) are essential for your brain and skin.

"Skipping meals helps you lose weight."

Fact: it can slow your metabolism and cause fatigue.

"Frozen produce is less nutritious."

Fact: Due to it being frozen at peak ripeness, it is also preserving the nutrient content.

"Organic food is always healthier."

Fact: The difference is that organic foods are farmed with different farming practices which avoid pesticides and fertilizers. Diversifying your diet by eating different fruits and vegatables are key to a healthy diet.
