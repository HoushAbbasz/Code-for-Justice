Malnutrition is a condition that occurs when the body does not receive the right balance of nutrients it needs to function properly. It includes both undernutrition and overnutrition, and it affects people of all ages and lifestyles.

It is a major global health concern and can lead to long-term effects that impact energy levels, immunity, cognitive health, and overall well-being.

  • Undernutrition ‚Äì insufficient intake of calories, protein, or essential nutrients.
  • Overnutrition ‚Äì excess calorie intake or diets high in unhealthy foods.
  • Micronutrient Deficiencies ‚Äì lack of specific vitamins or minerals like iron, B12, or vitamin D.

üîç Types of Malnutrition

Malnutrition appears in several forms, each impacting the body differently. Knowing these types helps with recognition and prevention.

  • Protein-Energy Malnutrition ‚Äì inadequate intake of calories or protein.
  • Micronutrient Deficiency ‚Äì low levels of essential vitamins/minerals.
  • Overnutrition ‚Äì consuming too many calories or unhealthy foods.
  • Dietary Imbalance ‚Äì calories present, but nutrients missing.

‚ö†Ô∏è Causes of Malnutrition

Malnutrition develops from a mix of medical, environmental, and lifestyle factors that influence nutrient intake or absorption.

  • Poor diet quality
  • Chronic illnesses
  • Digestive conditions
  • Food insecurity
  • Poverty or unstable housing
  • Mental health challenges
  • Medications affecting appetite

‚ö° Risk Factors

Certain groups are more likely to experience malnutrition due to medical or lifestyle circumstances.

  • Older adults
  • Individuals with chronic medical conditions
  • People experiencing food insecurity
  • People with restricted diets
  • Athletes with high nutrient needs

ü©∫ Signs & Symptoms

Symptoms vary depending on the type and severity of malnutrition, but can affect both physical and mental health.

Fatigue, weak muscles, brittle nails, hair thinning, slow healing, dizziness, weight changes.

Poor concentration, irritability, anxiety, memory issues, low motivation.

Pale skin, numbness, bone pain, cracked lips, vision issues, muscle cramps.

üí• Health Consequences

Untreated malnutrition affects immunity, muscle strength, daily energy, and disease risk.

  • Weak immune system
  • Muscle loss
  • Chronic fatigue
  • Higher disease risk
  • Cognitive issues
  • Bone loss

üß™ How Malnutrition Is Diagnosed

Doctors diagnose malnutrition through exams, lab tests, and medical history evaluation.

  • Physical exams
  • Blood tests
  • Body composition tests
  • Health history review
  • Digestive function tests

‚úÖ Preventing Malnutrition

Prevention focuses on accessible food, stable health, and early awareness.

  • Regular Health Monitoring: Detect deficiencies early.
  • Address Medical Conditions: Manage chronic illnesses.
  • Access to Nutritious Foods: Use community resources.
  • Food Safety: Prevent contamination.
  • Meal Planning: Include multiple food groups.
  • Mental Health Support: Maintain appetite stability.
  • Education: Learn nutrient needs.

üìû When to Seek Help

Seek help when symptoms persist or worsen.

  • Unexplained weight changes
  • Persistent fatigue
  • Low appetite
  • Frequent illness
  • Digestive issues
  • Hair, skin, or nail changes
